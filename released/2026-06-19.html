The Pulse · Story of the day

New methods quantify ventilatory control in sleep apnoea from routine polysomnography

Recent advances enable computable estimation of loop gain (LG) from signals available in standard polysomnography. LG values >1 indicate unstable ventilation, a key factor in apnoea. This shift to non-invasive measurement opens new avenues for diagnosis and treatment. Useful when refining diagnostic protocols for sleep apnoea.

Home-based melatonin onset profiles identify real-world circadian phenotypes

In a clinical population, self-collected salivary assessments of dim light melatonin onset (DLMO) identified distinct circadian phenotypes. Cross-sectional study; no code available yet. Useful when refining patient stratification for chronotherapy.

CPAP improves blood sugar control in patients with prediabetes and OSA

A review finds that continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) can improve glycaemic control in individuals with both prediabetes and obstructive sleep apnoea. Self-reported data; useful when assessing CPAP's role in managing comorbid conditions.

PVT sleepiness scores predict daytime alertness in OSA patients

In a study of 120 obstructive sleep apnoea (OSA) patients, psychomotor vigilance test (PVT) scores correlated significantly with EEG-observed current sleepiness. The PVT could serve as a non-invasive tool to assess daytime alertness in OSA management. Cross-sectional data; useful when revising your treatment protocols for adult patients with persistent OSA.

Altered HRV linked to higher mortality in COMISA patients

In 5225 participants from the Sleep Heart Health Study, altered heart rate variability (HRV) during wakefulness and sleep predicted all-cause mortality in those with co-morbid insomnia and obstructive sleep apnoea. Cross-sectional data; useful when assessing autonomic burden in COMISA patients.

Overnight sleep patterns predict morning brain metabolism in older adults

In a study of 142 older adults, overnight slow-wave activity and spindle density correlated with next-morning glucose metabolism. Cross-sectional design limits causality claims. Measure these sleep features when assessing cognitive decline risk.

Text messages reduce fatigue in emergency medical staff

A cluster-randomised trial shows real-time text message interventions mitigate workplace fatigue among emergency medical services personnel. Self-reported data suggests a significant reduction in fatigue levels after the intervention. Useful when revising protocols for shift workers to improve patient care and staff well-being.
