Machine learning predicts Parkinson's risk in RBD patients

A new machine-learning model diagnoses and classifies isolated REM sleep behaviour disorder (RBD), predicting phenoconversion to Parkinson’s disease. Atomoxetine combo therapy cuts OSA severity in meta-analysis

A meta-analysis of five randomized placebo-controlled trials found atomoxetine combination therapy reduced OSA severity by an average Apnoea-Hypopnoea Index (AHI) reduction of 10 events per hour. Observational, so causal link unproven. Read to update your treatment options for OSA.

Cognitive impairment linked to higher OSA prevalence in adults

A meta-analysis across multiple studies reveals that cognitive impairment is associated with a significantly higher prevalence of obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) among adults. This finding may influence future clinical practice guidelines and research priorities. Self-reported data, so confirmation needed.

Fingertip wearable accurately diagnoses OSA and tracks CPAP response in split-night trials

A fingertip device validated on 120 patients accurately diagnosed obstructive sleep apnoea (OSA) and tracked continuous positive airway pressure (CPAP) treatment response during split-night polysomnography. The device matched expert staging for OSA severity but lacks real-world validation yet. Worth piloting against your PSG cohort to assess integration into clinical practice.

Restless legs syndrome more common in primary hyperparathyroidism

A case-control study found restless legs syndrome (RLS) is more frequent in patients with primary hyperparathyroidism. This observational link aids diagnosis but causality remains unproven. Worth adding RLS to the screening checklist for suspected hyperparathyroidism.

Pink noise and earplugs cut sleep disruption from various noises equally well

In a study comparing pink noise and earplugs, both methods effectively reduced sleep disturbance caused by different environmental noises. No significant differences were found between the two interventions. Vendor-supplied data. Worth adding either to your sleep hygiene protocol for patients with noise issues.

Partner disturbance in co-sleeping disrupts sleep architecture across three studies

A systematic review of 37 studies found that partner disturbances significantly altered sleep stages and quality, impacting deep sleep and REM cycles. Cross-study synthesis limited by heterogeneity. Discuss these findings when tailoring sleep hygiene advice for couples.
