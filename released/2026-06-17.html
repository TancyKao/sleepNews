AASM recommends combining CBT-I with hypnotics for chronic insomnia

The AASM now advises clinicians to pair cognitive-behavioural therapy for insomnia (CBT-I) with short-term use of hypnotic medication for chronic insomnia, marking a shift from previous guidelines. Useful when revising your treatment protocols for adult patients with persistent insomnia.

A study of former National Football League players found those with obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) had a significantly increased risk for cardiovascular disease. Observational study, no causal link proven. Discuss at your next risk assessment protocol review.

A proof-of-concept trial shows therapeutic oxygen is more effective than medical air for central sleep apnea in infants with Prader-Willi syndrome. Useful when revising treatment protocols for young patients.

A double-blind trial found lemborexant significantly improved WASO (time awake after falling asleep) by 27 minutes versus placebo. Useful when revising treatment protocols for night-shift workers, though no code or further data release details are available.
