Oxybates effective in narcolepsy with comorbid mental health disorders

A study of 120 patients found oxybates improved excessive daytime sleepiness and cataplexy without exacerbating psychiatric conditions. Double-blind, placebo-controlled trial. Useful when revising treatment protocols for narcolepsy patients with comorbidity.

High-frequency brain waves differ in parasomnia and hypermotor epilepsy

A comparative analysis of high-frequency oscillations across wakefulness and sleep shows distinct patterns in NREM parasomnia versus sleep-related hypermotor epilepsy. This study provides actionable data for clinicians diagnosing these conditions, though the cross-sectional design limits causal inference. Useful when refining diagnostic protocols for patients with both conditions.

Amplitude and phase markers identified in 120 participants correlate with the frequency and duration of neurological events during sleep. Preliminary, cross-sectional study. Useful when refining diagnostic protocols for patients suspected of having neurological sleep apnea.

Machine learning spots coronary microvascular dysfunction with ECG features

Using VCG signals from 82 CMD patients and 252 controls, this study extracted temporal-spatial heterogeneity indices and entropy metrics. Sequential backward selection identified key features for detection. Cross-sectional design limits causality. Useful when refining diagnostic protocols for CMD.
